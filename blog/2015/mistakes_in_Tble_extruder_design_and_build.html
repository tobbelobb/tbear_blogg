Notes to self: Mistakes in Tble-extuder design and build

  • There was a 2 mm typo making the extrusion miss the entrance of the hot end.
  • Very imprecise prints led to a lot of gear sanding.
  • Design called for almost impossibly accurate control over hobbed bold width (so hot end width would match and be locked in place).
  • Mounting the built drive required screwing self-tapping screws in almost inaccessible places.
  • To make the large gear fit, I had to cut away a large part of the drive leg (where there is a hole in CAD, see previous post).
  • Many nut locks were missing in CAD model.
  • Holes supporting 623 bearings should be wide enough to let M3 nut through.
  • Didn't know how to any good ways to mount 5 mm inner diameter hobbed bolt on M3 screw. Landed on solution: Grind down two nyloc nuts and use lots of super glue.
