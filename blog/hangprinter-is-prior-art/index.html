Prior Art Relevant to Patent US 11230032

2-2-2022

The patent in question was filed on Apr 13, 2018. We will refer to the patented contraption as SkyBAAM for short. We will present prior art relevant to SkyBAAM, all of which was unmentioned and unreferenced in the patent. The prior art we present will mostly have been published within context of the open source project called Hangprinter.

The patent made only two references to Hangprinter:

  • The Wikipedia Article
  • An out of date article from 3ders.org

Those two sources alone do not cover enough details to reveal how deeply similar SkyBAAM is to the pre-existing Hangprinter.

First Of All

The patent contains many ideas that have been explored, published, and then discontinued within the Hangprinter community.

The Hangprinter prototype that was most visually similar to Fig 4 in the patent might be this early version 3 prototype, that was published and described in detail on April 4, 2017.

However, the similarities are not merely superficial. This text will show similarities on the conceptual level in the form of ideas that were formulated, and sometimes also implemented within the Hangprinter Project, before they were reiterated in the patent.

Prior Art Published in the Repo

The main Hangprinter repo is the heart of the Hangprinter project.

Hangprinter is developed following open source methodology. This means that all source files are published to the repo, freely for anyone to use, for any purpose, soon after they are created. They are also re-uploaded soon after any modification is made.

The re-uploads do not overwrite old work. Instead, each change is registered in an elaborate version control system (git/Github), that presents the newest version to the user, as well as saving and catalouging every previous version in chronological order.

The "source files" include, but are not limited to

  • CAD drawings
  • Source code for programming the firmware of any microcontrollers involved
  • Assembly and usage instructions
  • A copyright license

The version controlled, and publicly available directory is called "the repo". It can be found here.

Was The Repo Publicly Available?

Anyone had access and usage rights to the repo on Apr 13, 2018, if they could find the link. Because of a wave of media attention in 2017, the link to the Hangprinter repo was widely spread out, across hundreds of websites.

Information about how popular and well known the repo was on Apr 13, 2018 can be found by using archive.org, also known as Wayback Machine. It is service that stores and archives old web pages, so we can visit them again, and see exactly how they looked on given dates.

This is how github.com/tobbelobb/hangprinter looked on Jun 26, 2017:

In the image we can see that the repo had 77 watchers, 248 stars, and 49 forks. This means that this was a popular repo at the time. The 49 forks means that at least 49 people have copied Hangprinter's source files to use as the basis for their own cable driven robot build.

The license in the repo allows and encourages such copying. Hangprinter was the most popular and widely known basis for cable driven robot development in 2017 and 2018.

I will re-post some relevant images and quotes here. Not all quotes are mine, but all images are mine. All were posted between Apr 23, 2014 and Nov 14 2017. Further details are in the forum thread.

Every possible combination of wire-lengths, (l0, l1, l2), corresponds to a unique point in the avilable print area (a triangle). To map a point (x', y') to the corresponding wire-lengths, all we have to do is place the wires anchor-points in the cartesian coordinate system, and compute the lengths from each anchor point to the point (x', y').
